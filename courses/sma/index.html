Overview

Description:  The course will cover techniques and algorithms to analyze the structure of social networks and identify their main properties. We start by studying the central concepts of social media analytics (e.g., data structures, similarity/centrality measures, traversal algorithms, etc.). Next, the course will delve into studying various algorithms to identify communities in social networks. Then, the course will cover advanced social network applications, including diffusion/influence, social recommendation, graph completion,  graph mining, and link analysis.

Learning outcomes: On successful completion of this course, you will be able to:

  • Collect and store social media networks and identify their main properties
  • Apply clustering techniques to detect social communities
  • Optimize information diffusion on social networks
  • Boost recommendation systems using social networks
  • Apply matrix factorization/decomposition techniques to extract latent information

Structure

Teaching format: This course consists of lectures and exercises/labs. The weekly/bi-weekly exercises are an important part of the course.

Textbook: The textbook for the course is Social Data Mining: an Introduction, First edition, Cambridge University Press, Reza Zafarani, Mohammad Ali Abbasi and Huan Liu, 2014

Exercises: The exercises will be given by Manuel Mondal. It is highly recommended to solve the exercises before attending the exercise session. Solving the exercises will be the best way to prepare for the final exam.
